Sprint 60 (version 18)

Designing an 'Apply' service New journey to be more specific when signposting users

Personas
  • Sarah Green
  • 1st Line of Address = 13 Palm Road
  • Postcode = NR33 4GP
  • Date of birth = 01 / 01 / 1999 (Over 18 - No Benefit Match)
  • National Insurance number = MN 98 75 44 G

Hypothesis A

We believe that giving users the option of uploading evidence online will be a useful feature for any user who we have been unable to give an instant decision to as it will allow them join the scheme if they information provided proves they should be eligible. This will reduce the amount of people contacting our contact centre to challenge their decision.

Background:

  • Currently only users who are in receipt of Universal Credit (and whose family’s monthly ‘take-home pay’ is £408 or less and are pregnant or have children under 4) or Child Tax Credits (and whose family’s annual income is £16,190 or less and have children under 4) are able to apply online.
  • This means that users receiving other legacy benefits can’t.
  • Currently, people who receive other forms of legacy benefits (Income Support, Jobseeker’s Allowance, Pension Credit, Working Tax Credit, Child Tax Credit and Employment and Support Allowance) are asked to email or phone us, and to confirm their eligibility, we then ask them to provide evidence by email or post.
  • The goal of the service is to allow people on any eligible legacy benefit, to be able to apply online directly to us, thus bypassing the need to call or email our back office staff, which should also reduce calls to the contact centre by 20% for the top 3 call drivers.

What was designed:

  • An in-depth apply journey for healthy start applicants to fill in their personal details, children’s details, legacy benefit details and pregnancy details in order to apply to receive Healthy Start.

Designing an 'Apply' service Data matching and better sign-posting for our users

Personas
  • Sarah Green
  • 1st Line of Address = 13 Palm Road
  • Postcode = NR33 4GP
  • Date of birth = 01 / 01 / 2007 (under 16)
  • Date of birth = 01 / 01 / 2005 (under 18)
  • Date of birth = 01 / 01 / 1999 (over 18 - not on UC)
  • National Insurance number = MN 98 75 44 G

Hypothesis A

We believe that users need more guidance and support in knowing what to do if they are not able to get through a successful online application. If the user is under 16, 16 to 18 or 18+ and failed the online applcication they will need to know what their next steps are.

Hypothesis B

We believe that issues with matching data could potentially be due to names not being entered correctly i.e. middle names being entered into the first name input. User will likely match if they have the option to enter a middle name if needed.

What we created:

  • Under 16 would be signposted as soon as the DOB was captured, as they would not continue to the National insurance number page
  • Aged 16 to 18 would complete a benefit check and then be signposted if no match was made
  • Over 18 would complete a benefit check and then be signposted if no match was made
  • Name screens i.e. Applicants name and Childrens name, to include a middle name input to help towards first time matching

What we plan to test:

  • That all users understand what they need to do next if they get a signposting screen during their online application
  • That users are able to add their middle names (if required) to the input as well as any children middle names and continue the application

What we found:

  • Testing was carried out for the over 18 signposting screen which tested very well, participants understood the information we provided on these screens and what to do next
  • Under 16 and ages 16 to 18 still require usability testing
  • Data matching was investigated as an API issue and the middle name input was no longer required

Any known issues?

  • When a participant reached the signposting screen, they mentioned that they would return to the previous screens to check what they had entered into the personal details screens, this however was not possible as all session data was cleared once the signpost screen was reached, we now needed to think of a way of allowing that to happen

  • Jane Yellow (on universal credit)
  • National Insurance number = JN 33 35 46 K

  • 1st Line of Address = 13 Palm Road
  • Postcode = NR33 4GT
  • Pregnant? = YES or NO
  • Children Under 1? = NO
  • Children Between 1 & 4? = NO

Hypothesis A

We believe that giving under 18s the opportunity to apply online for help to buy food, milk and vitamins, will be an improved user experience, allowing under 18s to upload their evidence through the online application rather than needing to phone in and be told to email evidence, a second potential online journey was to allow under 18s to apply online and then putting these in a pending state while they email over evidence, this will result in less calls to the call centre and allowing the under 18 to apply at a time that suits them. A business need was to prevent Scottish citizens from applying, the short term fix for this we believe is to ask whether the applicant is from England, Wales or Northern Ireland, to potentially signpost scottish applicants to Best Start Foods.

What we created:

  • The file upload journey, being the more appealing journey was created to allow under 18s to go through the online application then directly upload their evidence through file upload functionality, cutting out the need to call or post evidence
  • The second journey for under 18s was to allow these to go through the application and receive all information about sending in evidence at the end but having their application in a pending state until the evidence is received and checked, again meaning they won't need to call to complete the applicatioin
  • We also created a new screen which was at the very beginning of the service which would ask the user if they lived in England, Wales or Northern Ireland, this was put in place to help prevent any other citizens from applying to the service. We also used this to help signpost Scottish citizens to the Scotland version, Best Start

What we plan to test:

  • We plan to test if the file upload journey is beneficial to the user and how well they navigate through the journey but also adding the evidnece to be uploaded
  • We plan to test how users feel about going through the second under 18 journey and being required to send in their evidence after completing the application
  • We plan to test how easily users navigate through the England, Wales and Northern Ireland screen and if this makes sense and easy to understand

What we found:

  • The under 18s online journey was put on hold and are continuing to have these apply through contacting the call center
  • Preventing Scottish citizens from applying was an urgent business need and did not make it through usability testing

Any known issues?

  • If file upload functionality was implemented, we would need a way to make all files accessible to the internal staff

For all design thinking up to this point, EE have put together a helpful list of design rationales.

What we created:

  • Used the 'NHS Hub' pattern as the starting point for entering the service. This replicates the potential journey of an applicant entering the service via the NHS website. Idea is to use this content consistantly across all 3 entry points; GOV.uk, NHS.uk and nhsbsa.nhs.uk
  • On the Low Income Scheme (another BSA service) we found that 16.4% of adults in England (or 1 in 6 adults) can be described as having ‘very poor literacy skills’. Because of this, users found it extremely helpful when we personalised / tailored the user journey depending on who is applying (main benificiary / third party). So given users of the Low Income Scheme and Healthy Start are from a similar demographic - it makes sense to provide a similar approach here. This is likely to come in very useful further down the line when we potentially need to know about Partner / Carer details.
  • Replaced the EE pattern of selecting number of children under 4 years old via radio buttons, with the tested NHS BSA pattern for 'Adding another' e.g. 1. Do you have any children under 4 years old? 2. Enter child details 3. Do you have another child under 4 years old? (↫ loop).
  • Whilst we understand how achievable checking DWP / HMRC data is, we have added in additional questions in regards to the selected benefits e.g. Universal Credit (Was your take-home pay for your last Universal Credit period £408 or less?), Child Tax Credit (Is your household income £16,190 or less?), Jobseekers Allowance (Which type of Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA) do you get?), Employment and Support Allowance (Which type of Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) do you get?), Working Tax Credit (Is your family receiving the 4 week 'run-on' payment?).
  • Changed the flow slightly so that we ask; Are you pregnant? and Do you have any children under 4 years old? after each other. If the answer to any of these is 'Yes' we then ask for further details.
