We cannot complete your Healthy Start application online

If you get Universal Credit, follow these steps and apply again online.

1. Check your details

If you’re more than 10 weeks pregnant or have children under the age of 4, go to your Universal Credit account to check how your details are listed on your Universal Credit claim.

2. Make sure your details match

When you apply for Healthy Start, the details you give us must match exactly with the details on your Universal Credit claim. This includes your:

  • name
  • date of birth
  • address, including postcode
  • children's names and dates of birth

3. If you’re pregnant, check your due date

We use the estimated due date that you give us to work out if you're more than 10 weeks pregnant.

If you're less than 10 weeks pregnant and have no other children under the age of 4, you'll need to apply again after you've completed your tenth week of pregnancy.

I do not get Universal Credit but I think I’m eligible for Healthy Start

You may be eligible for Healthy Start if you’re more than 10 weeks pregnant and you:

  • are under 18
  • get Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)

You may also be eligible if you’re more than 10 weeks pregnant or have at least one child under 4, and you get:

  • Child Tax Credit
  • Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • Income Support
  • Pension Credit
