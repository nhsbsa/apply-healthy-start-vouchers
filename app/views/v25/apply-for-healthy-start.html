Apply for Healthy Start

When you apply for Healthy Start online, we’ll ask for:

  • your name, date of birth and address
  • your National Insurance number
  • your estimated due date (if you’re pregnant)
  • the names and dates of birth of any children under 4 years old

If you’re applying for any children under 4, make sure no one else is already receiving Healthy Start payments for them.

Apply online

You can apply online if you get:

  • Universal Credit and your monthly take home pay is £408 or less
  • Child Tax Credit and you have at least one child under 4 – your annual household income must be £16, 190 or less

If you get Child Tax Credit and all your children are aged 4 or older, you need to apply by email or phone for a new pregnancy if your annual household income is £16, 190.

Important: Universal Credit claimants

The details you use to apply for Healthy Start must match the details on your Universal Credit claim.

Go to your Universal Credit account to check:

  • your address details, including your postcode
  • all of your children under 4 are named on your Universal Credit claim

Apply by email or phone

You’ll need to apply by email or phone for your pregnancy or children under 4 if you get:

  • Income-based Jobseekers Allowance (JSA)
  • Income Support
  • Pension Credit including the Child Addition

If you’re more than 10 weeks pregnant, you can apply for payments until your baby is born if you:

  • are under 18 and do not get any other benefits
  • get Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
Information:

Email: healthy.start@nhsbsa.nhs.uk

Telephone: 0300 330 7010 Phone lines are open Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm (except public holidays).

Find out more about call charges (opens in a new tab).

If you are not a British citizen but your child is

If you’ve checked your eligibility, you need to ask for an application form by emailing healthystartclaim@dhsc.gov.uk

You should only use this email address if you cannot claim public funds because of your immigration status.
