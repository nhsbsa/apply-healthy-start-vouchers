How to get help to buy food and milk with NHS Healthy Start: step by step

NHS Healthy Start can help you with the cost of buying healthy food and milk.

If you’re eligible, you’ll get:

  • £4.25 each week from your tenth week of pregnancy
  • £8.50 per week for children from birth to one year old
  • £4.25 each week for children aged between 1 and 4 years old

You can use this service to make a new Healthy Start application or manage your existing claim.

Before you start a new application, check if you can apply online.

  1. Step 1 Check you’re eligible for Healthy Start

    You can apply for Healthy Start if you’re more than 10 weeks pregnant or have at least 1 child under 4 years old, and you get:

    • Universal Credit and your family’s monthly ‘take-home pay’ is £408 or less
    • Child Tax Credit with an annual household income of £16,190 or less, and you do not also get Working Tax Credit
    • Income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
    • Income Support
    • Pension Credit (including the child addition)

    If you’re more than 10 weeks pregnant, you can apply for Healthy Start payments until your estimated due date if you:

    • are under 18 and do not get any benefits
    • get Income-related Employment and Support Allowance

    If you are not a British citizen but your child is

    You might be eligible for Healthy Start depending on your immigration status.

    You can get Healthy Start if all the following are true:

    • you have at least one British child under 4 years old – check if your child is automatically a British citizen (opens in a new tab) if you’re not sure
    • your family earns £408 or less per month after tax
    • you cannot claim ‘public funds’ (opens in a new tab) like benefits – either because of your immigration status or because you do not have an immigration status

    Your biometric residence permit (BRP) or your online immigration status (opens in a new tab) will say if you cannot claim public funds. You might also have a letter from the Home Office about it.

  2. Step 2 Apply for Healthy Start

    If you get Universal Credit or Child Tax Credit

    Most people who get Universal Credit or Child tax Credit can apply online.

    Important: Universal Credit claimants

    The details you use to apply for Healthy Start must match the details on your Universal Credit claim.

    Go to your Universal Credit account (opens in a new tab) to check:

    • your address details, including your postcode
    • all of your children are named on your Universal Credit claim
    Child Tax Credit claimants

    You can apply online if you:

    • have at least one child under 4
    • are more than 10 weeks pregnant and have at least one child under the age of 4

    If you are more than 10 weeks pregnant and all your other children are aged 4 or older, you’ll need to ask for an application form by phone or email.

    If you do not get Universal Credit or Child Tax Credit

    If you are eligible for Healthy Start but you do not get Universal Credit or Child Tax Credit, you’ll need to contact us by email or phone so we can send you an application form.

    Email: healthy.start@nhsbsa.nhs.uk
    We aim to respond within 2 working days

    Phone: 0300 330 7010
    Phone lines are open Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm (excluding bank holidays).

    Find out more about call charges (opens in a new tab).

    If you are not a British citizen but your child is

    To apply for Healthy Start, ask for an application form by emailing healthy.start@nhsbsa.nhs.uk

    Only use this email address if you think you cannot claim public funds because of your immigration status.

  3. Step 3 Activate your card

    Before you use your card for the first time, call 0300 330 2090 to activate your card and find out your PIN.

    When you call, you’ll need your:

    • card
    • date of birth
    • client ID – you can find this on the top of the letter that comes with your card

    When you’ve activated your card, we’ll send your PIN by email or text message. If you did not give us your email address or mobile phone number when you applied, we’ll post your PIN out 2 days after we send your card.

  4. Step 4 Keep your details up to date

    Your Healthy Start payments will change depending on:

    • your children’s ages
    • if you’re pregnant
    • your income and benefits

    To make sure you need to let us know as soon as possible if your circumstances change and you need to update your details. Let us know if you need to:

    • add a new pregnancy
    • add a new baby or child
    • update your personal details – for example, your name or address
    • update your children’s details

  5. Step 5 Tell us if your card is lost, damaged or stolen

    Call 0300 330 2090 to report your card as lost, damaged or stolen.

    This is an automated phone line that’s 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

    You can also use this number to:

    • activate your card
    • get your PIN
    • hear your balance

    Find out more about call charges (opens in a new tab).

    If you’re Deaf or have a hearing loss

    Our automated phone line is not accessible if you’re Deaf or have a hearing loss or tinnitus.

    You can call us on 0300 330 7010

    This phone line is open Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm (excluding public holidays), and is compatible with assistive listening devices like personal hearing loops.
