Before you start

We use NHS and government data sources to find and update your Healthy Start claim.

Update your pregnancy or children’s details

If you’re already getting Healthy Start payments, you can use this service to tell us:

  • your expected due date if you’re pregnant
  • you’ve given birth
  • you’ve become the main carer for a child under 4

You cannot view or change any other details linked to your Healthy Start claim.

If you get Universal Credit

If you’re adding a new child under 4 to your Healthy Start claim, you need to tell Universal Credit about your new child (opens in a new tab) before we can start making payments.

Information:

Make sure the children’s details you give us exactly match how they appear on your Universal Credit claim (opens in a new tab).

Important: What you’ll need

We need to check your identity to keep your claim secure. You’ll need to enter:

  • your National Insurance number
  • a security code - we’ll send this to the mobile phone number you gave us when you applied for Healthy Start

GOV.UK can help you find your National Insurance number (opens in a new tab) if you do not remember it.

If you cannot find your National Insurance number or you do not have a mobile number linked to your Healthy Start account, you’ll need to contact us to update your claim.
