Shopping with your card

Your Healthy Start payments help you with the cost of buying fruit, vegetables, cow’s milk and infant formula.

This is to support you and your growing baby to stay healthy during your pregnancy, and to help give your children a balanced diet.

You can

  • buy fruit, vegetables, cow’s milk and infant formula
  • use your card to collect free Healthy Start vitamins
  • shop in places that accept Mastercard®

You can't

  • you cannot buy items that are not on the list of permitted food and milk
  • you cannot use your card online or in your phone’s wallet app
  • you cannot use your card outside of the UK, including in the Republic of Ireland
  • you cannot get cashback or withdraw money from a cashpoint

These guides list the permitted types of fruit, vegetables, milk and infant formula you can buy with your Healthy Start card.

Fruit and vegetables

You can buy fruit and vegetables that are:

  • fresh, frozen or tinned
  • whole or chopped
  • packaged or loose
  • in water or fruit juice

You can also buy fresh, dried or tinned pulses.

The fruit and vegetables you buy should not be:

  • with added ingredients like fat, oil, salt, sugar or flavourings – including products like chips or onion rings
  • juiced or pre-cooked
  • fruits in syrup
  • smoothies
Plain cow’s milk

You should buy plain cow’s milk which can be:

  • pasteurised
  • sterilised
  • long-life
  • ultra-heat treated (UHT)

It should not be:

  • flavoured
  • coloured
  • evaporated
  • condensed
  • plant-based milk
  • powdered (unless it’s stage one infant formula)
Infant formula

You should only buy infant formula that’s:

  • labelled as suitable from birth
  • made from cow’s milk

It should not be:

  • follow-on formula or milk
  • labelled as ‘from 6 months’ or ‘from 6 to 12 months’

Find out more about infant formula.

Where you can shop

You can use your card in most shops that:

  • sell fruit, vegetables and milk
  • accept Mastercard® – you can look for the Mastercard symbol on the shop door or by the till, or ask an employee in the shop if you’re not sure

This includes:

  • supermarkets
  • convenience stores and corner shops
  • grocery stores
  • chemists and pharmacies
  • markets
  • dairy products stores
  • newsagents and news stands
  • butcher’s shops
  • petrol stations

If you try to use your Healthy Start card anywhere else, it will be declined. For example, you cannot use your card in a fast food restaurant.

How to shop

You need to activate your card before you can use it. The first time you use your card, you’ll need to enter your PIN.

After you’ve used your PIN for the first time, you’ll be able to make contactless payments. To keep your card secure, we’ll ask you to enter your PIN if you’ve:

  • made 4 contactless payments in a row
  • spent a combined £150

You can contact us if you’d like to turn off contactless payments.

Splitting transactions

If you’re buying any items other than fruit, vegetables, milk or infant formula, you’ll need to split your shopping into 2 transactions at the till. You’ll need to use:

  • your Healthy Start card to pay for any fruit, vegetables, milk and infant formula
  • another card or cash to pay for any other items – even if they’re for your baby or children

Some shops might need to do this as separate transactions. At self-checkout machines, you can only split your payment between your Healthy Start card and cash, not another debit or credit card.

You can only spend the balance of your Healthy Start card. It does not have an overdraft function.

Do not worry if you make a mistake when using your Healthy Start card. It will not affect your benefit payments.

Refunds

If you need to get a refund on something you’ve bought, go back to the shop with your items, receipt and Healthy Start card. The money will be refunded to your Healthy Start card.
