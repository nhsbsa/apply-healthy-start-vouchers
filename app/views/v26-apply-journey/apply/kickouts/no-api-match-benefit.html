If you get Universal Credit

Go to your Universal Credit account (opens in a new tab) to check how you’ve entered your:

  • name
  • address, including postcode
  • date of birth

The details on your Healthy Start application need to match exactly with how they’re listed on your Universal Credit claim.

If you get Child Tax Credit

You need to check the details on your Healthy Start application match exactly with how they’re listed your Child Tax Credit letter

If you’re under 18 or get a different benefit

You can only apply online if you get Universal Credit or Child Tax Credit.

If you’re under 18 and more than 10 weeks pregnant or you get a different qualifying benefit, you’ll need to contact us to apply.

Check if you get a qualifying benefit

You can contact us to apply for Healthy Start if you’re more than 10 weeks pregnant or have at least one child under 4 years old and you get:

  • Income-based Job Seeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • Income Support
  • Pension Credit (including the child addition)
Information:

You can get Healthy Start payments if you’re under 18 and more than 10 weeks pregnant - you do not need to get any benefits.

If you get Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA), you can apply for Healthy Start if you’re more than 10 weeks pregnant. Your Healthy Start payments will stop when you reach your due date.
