Update your details

You can update your details online if you currently get Healthy Start payments and either:

  • Universal Credit
  • Child Tax Credit

It’s important to keep your details up to date so we can make sure you’re being paid correctly.

If you do not get Universal Credit or Child Tax Credit

You cannot update your details online.

You need to contact us by phone or email to tell us about any changes in your circumstances if you currently get Healthy Start payments and get:

  • Income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Income Support
  • Pension Credit with the child addition

Tell us about a new pregnancy or child

You can update your claim online if you’re currently getting Healthy Start payments and you need to add a new:

  • pregnancy
  • baby or child – you may have recently given birth, or become responsible for an older child under 4

Universal Credit claimants

If you’re adding a new child under 4 to your Healthy Start claim, you need to tell Universal Credit about your new child (opens in a new tab) before we can start making payments. 

If you need to make another change

Let us know by phone or email if there’s any other changes in circumstances that might affect your Healthy Start claim. For example, if you need to:

  • change your name or address
  • change the name of any children on your claim
  • stop your payments for a pregnancy or a child
Information:

Email: healthy.start@nhsbsa.nhs.uk

Phone: 0300 330 7010

Phone lines are open 8am to 6pm, Monday to Friday (except public holidays).

Find out more about call charges (opens in a new tab).

If your card has been lost, damaged or stolen

Call 0300 330 2090 if your card has been lost, damaged or stolen. When you call, you’ll need your:

  • date of birth
  • 10 digit client ID – you can find this on any letters, emails or text messages we’ve sent you

This is an automated phone line that’s available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

If you’re Deaf or have a hearing loss

Our automated phone line is not accessible if you’re Deaf or have a hearing loss or tinnitus.

You can call us on 0300 330 7010 and use the:

  • Relay app (opens in a new tab)
  • Relay textphone service (opens in a new tab)

This phone line is open Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm (except public holidays).
